# LOCAL CHAPTER STARTUP GUIDE
## Building The Human Standard Movement in Your Community

---

## WELCOME

Thank you for your interest in starting a local Human Standard chapter. Grassroots organizing is essential to building the movement that can transform ideas into policy. This guide provides everything you need to get started.

---

## PART 1: GETTING STARTED

### Step 1: Understand the Material

Before organizing others, deeply understand The Human Standard:

**Required Reading:**
- MANIFESTO.md - Core principles and vision
- EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY.md - Quick overview
- Citizen's Royalty_POLICY_FRAMEWORK.md - Key policy details
- FAQ.md - Common questions and answers

**Recommended Reading:**
- AI_GOVERNANCE_FRAMEWORK.md
- PHILOSOPHICAL_FOUNDATION.md
- CROSS_PARTISAN_MESSAGING.md

### Step 2: Find Your Founding Team

A chapter needs 3-5 committed founders. Look for people who are:
- Genuinely interested in the ideas
- Willing to commit time regularly
- Connected to different communities
- Reliable and organized

**Where to find them:**
- Friends and family who share concerns about automation
- Coworkers interested in economic policy
- Social media connections
- Local policy discussion groups
- University economics/philosophy departments

### Step 3: Register Your Chapter

Email chapters@thehumanstandard.org with:
- City/region name
- Founder names and contact info
- Brief description of your community
- Your goals for the first 3 months

You will receive:
- Official chapter recognition
- Access to organizer resources
- Connection to other chapter leaders
- Promotional materials

## PART 2: ORGANIZING STRUCTURE

### Chapter Roles

**Coordinator** (Required)
- Primary contact with national organization
- Facilitates meetings
- Ensures follow-through on commitments

**Communications Lead**
- Manages social media
- Writes local content
- Handles media inquiries

**Events Lead**
- Plans and executes events
- Manages logistics
- Coordinates volunteers

**Outreach Lead**
- Recruits new members
- Builds coalitions
- Represents chapter externally

**Treasurer** (if handling money)
- Manages chapter funds
- Ensures financial transparency
- Files any required reports

### Decision-Making

**Consensus preferred, voting when needed:**
1. Discuss proposals openly
2. Address concerns and modify
3. Seek consensus
4. If no consensus, simple majority vote
5. Record decisions in meeting notes

## PART 3: ACTIVITIES

### Monthly Meeting Template

**Duration**: 90 minutes

**Agenda:**
1. Welcome and introductions (10 min)
2. Policy deep-dive or guest speaker (30 min)
3. Discussion and Q&A (20 min)
4. Organizational business (15 min)
5. Action items and next steps (10 min)
6. Socializing (remaining time)

**Tips:**
- Start and end on time
- Rotate facilitation
- Take notes and share
- Always have food and drinks
- Create welcoming environment

### Educational Events

**Policy Discussion Series**
- Monthly focus on different Human Standard policies
- Invite local experts as guest speakers
- Encourage debate and questions

**Book and Article Club**
- Read and discuss related works
- Connect academic ideas to Human Standard

**Film Screenings**
- Documentaries on automation, Citizen's Royalty, AI
- Discussion afterward

**Public Lectures**
- Host speakers on automation economy
- Partner with universities, libraries

### Outreach Activities

**Tabling**
- Farmers markets
- Community events
- University campuses
- Provide one-pagers and sign-up sheets

**Community Presentations**
- Rotary clubs, civic organizations
- Religious congregations
- Union meetings
- Business associations

**Coalition Building**
- Connect with aligned local groups
- Labor organizations
- Environmental groups
- Tech worker organizations
- Anti-poverty advocates

### Political Engagement

**Voter Education**
- Candidate forums on automation and Citizen's Royalty
- Voter guides on relevant issues
- Non-partisan issue education

**Official Outreach**
- Meet with local elected officials
- Present to city councils
- Engage state legislators
- Document positions on issues

**Testimony**
- Testify at relevant hearings
- Submit written comments
- Bring member voices to officials

## PART 4: COMMUNICATIONS

### Social Media

**Platforms (prioritize based on local audience):**
- Facebook (community building)
- Twitter/X (policy discussion)
- Instagram (visual content)
- TikTok (younger audience)
- Nextdoor (hyper-local)

**Posting Guidelines:**
- 3-5 posts per week minimum
- Mix: events, policy content, engagement
- Respond to comments within 24 hours
- Share national content with local perspective
- Use consistent branding

**Hashtags:**
- #HumanStandard
- #Citizen's Royaltynow
- #AutomationForAll
- #TechServesHumanity
- [Your city or region]

### Local Media

**Build relationships with:**
- Local newspaper reporters
- TV news producers
- Radio hosts
- Podcast creators
- Local bloggers

**Story angles:**
- Local automation impacts
- Community responses to economic insecurity
- Events and activities
- Local voices on policy

### Internal Communication

**Tools:**
- Signal or WhatsApp for quick coordination
- Email list for announcements
- Shared document folder (Google Drive or Dropbox)
- Calendar for events

## PART 5: SUSTAINABILITY

### Member Engagement

**New member onboarding:**
1. Welcome email with key resources
2. Personal call from coordinator
3. Invitation to next event
4. Assignment of mentor if interested
5. Follow-up after first event

**Retention:**
- Regular communication
- Meaningful roles for active members
- Recognition of contributions
- Social events (not just policy)
- Listen to feedback and adapt

### Avoiding Burnout

**Share the load:**
- Rotate responsibilities
- Limit individual commitments
- Check in on well-being
- Celebrate wins

**Sustainable pace:**
- One major event per month is enough
- Quality over quantity
- Build for long term, not sprint

### Handling Conflict

**Prevention:**
- Clear expectations
- Transparent processes
- Address issues early
- Focus on ideas, not personalities

**Resolution:**
- Private conversation first
- Mediation if needed
- Document if serious
- Contact national for support if required
