## Brand Overview

### Brand Essence

**The Human Standard** represents a vision for human flourishing in the age of artificial intelligence. Our visual identity communicates:

- **Optimism** - Technology serving humanity
- **Accessibility** - Ideas for everyone, not just elites
- **Credibility** - Serious policy, rigorous thinking
- **Modernity** - Forward-looking, not nostalgic
- **Humanity** - People first, always

### Brand Personality

| Attribute | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| Intelligent | Clear, reasoned, evidence-based |
| Approachable | Warm, inclusive, not technocratic |
| Bold | Confident, ambitious, unapologetic |
| Trustworthy | Honest, transparent, reliable |
| Hopeful | Positive vision, not just critique |

## Logo

### Primary Logo

```
THE
HUMAN
STANDARD
    [H]
```

**Description:**
- Stacked wordmark "THE HUMAN STANDARD"
- Geometric "H" monogram symbol
- Clean, modern sans-serif typography
- Symbol can be used alone when brand is established

**Concept:**
The "H" symbol is constructed from clean geometric forms suggesting both the human figure and technological precision. The negative space creates a sense of openness and possibility.

### Logo Variations

**1. Primary Lockup (Stacked)**
- Full wordmark with symbol
- Used when space allows
- Preferred for formal applications

**2. Horizontal Lockup**
- "THE HUMAN STANDARD" on one line with symbol
- Used when horizontal space is available
- Good for headers and banners

**3. Symbol Only**
- "H" monogram alone
- Used when brand is established
- Good for social media avatars, favicons

**4. Wordmark Only**
- Text without symbol
- Used when simplicity required
- Good for dense layouts

### Clear Space

Minimum clear space around logo = height of "H" in wordmark

### Minimum Size

- Print: 1 inch wide (primary lockup)
- Digital: 120 pixels wide (primary lockup)
- Symbol alone: 0.5 inch / 48 pixels

### Logo Don'ts

- Don't stretch or distort
- Don't rotate
- Don't change colors outside palette
- Don't add effects (shadows, gradients, etc.)
- Don't place on busy backgrounds without contrast
- Don't rearrange elements
- Don't use unapproved lockups

**Success Green**
- HEX: #27AE60
- *Use: Positive indicators, success states*

**Calm Blue**
- HEX: #5DADE2
- *Use: Links, interactive elements*

### Color Usage Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use Human Blue as dominant color
- Use Optimism Gold for emphasis and CTAs
- Maintain strong contrast for accessibility
- Use white space generously

**Don't:**
- Use too many colors in one design
- Use light text on light backgrounds
- Sacrifice legibility for aesthetics
- Use colors not in the palette

### Accessibility

All color combinations must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards:
- Normal text: 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum
- Large text: 3:1 contrast ratio minimum
- Icons and UI elements: 3:1 minimum

**Approved Text Combinations:**
- Human Blue on White (16.4:1) ✓
- Human Blue on Warm Sand (12.1:1) ✓
- Foundation Gray on White (9.1:1) ✓
- White on Human Blue (16.4:1) ✓
- White on Future Teal (4.8:1) ✓

## Typography

### Primary Typeface: Inter

**Why Inter:**
- Clean, modern sans-serif
- Excellent legibility at all sizes
- Free and open-source (aligns with values)
- Wide language support
- Full range of weights

**Weights Used:**
- Light (300) - Minimal use, large display
- Regular (400) - Body text
- Medium (500) - Subheadings, emphasis
- Semi-Bold (600) - Headings
- Bold (700) - Strong headlines

### Secondary Typeface: Merriweather

**Why Merriweather:**
- Readable serif for long-form content
- Complements Inter well
- Free and open-source
- Good print characteristics

**Use Cases:**
- Long-form articles and reports
- Pull quotes and testimonials
- Traditional/formal applications

### Type Scale

**Display:**
- Display 1: 72px / Semi-Bold
- Display 2: 48px / Semi-Bold

**Headings:**
- H1: 36px / Semi-Bold
- H2: 28px / Semi-Bold
- H3: 22px / Medium
- H4: 18px / Medium

**Body:**
- Large: 18px / Regular
- Regular: 16px / Regular
- Small: 14px / Regular
- Caption: 12px / Regular

**Line Height:**
- Headings: 1.2
- Body text: 1.6
- Captions: 1.4

### Typography Rules

**Do:**
- Use consistent hierarchy
- Left-align body text
- Use adequate line length (45-75 characters)
- Allow generous line spacing

**Don't:**
- Use more than 2-3 weights per design
- Use all caps for long text
- Use decorative fonts
- Center long paragraphs

## Imagery

### Photography Style

**Subjects:**
- Diverse people of all ages, races, backgrounds
- Real environments, not staged studios
- Technology in human context
- Community and connection
- Work and purpose (various types)

**Treatment:**
- Natural lighting preferred
- Warm, optimistic color grading
- Not overly filtered or stylized
- High quality and resolution
- Authentic and documentary feel

**Avoid:**
- Stock photo clichés
- Isolated individuals looking sad
- Dystopian or menacing technology
- Exclusively young, urban, or coastal subjects
- Homogeneous groups

### Illustration Style

**Approach:**
- Clean, geometric, modern
- Optimistic and approachable
- Use brand color palette
- Abstract representation of concepts
- Consistent line weight and style

**Use Cases:**
- Data visualization
- Concept explanation
- Icon systems
- Report covers
- Social media graphics

### Iconography

**Style:**
- Simple, geometric line icons
- 2px stroke weight
- Rounded corners (2px radius)
- Single color or dual-tone
- From brand palette only

**Icon Sets Needed:**
- Policy topics (UBI, AI, automation, etc.)
- Actions (donate, volunteer, share, etc.)
- Navigation (menu, search, social, etc.)
- Status (success, error, warning, info)

## Design Elements

### Patterns

**Grid Pattern:**
- Subtle dot or line grid
- Suggests technology and precision
- Use as subtle background texture

**Human Pattern:**
- Abstract representation of connected people
- For community/coalition materials
- Low opacity backgrounds

### Data Visualization

**Charts and Graphs:**
- Use brand colors
- Clear labels and legends
- Minimize chartjunk
- Accessible with patterns (not just color)
- Source citations always included

**Infographics:**
- Clear visual hierarchy
- One key message per graphic
- Data accuracy paramount
- Shareable dimensions (1:1 or 16:9)

### Dividers and Lines

- 1-2px lines in Foundation Gray or Calm Blue
- Generous spacing around dividers
- Use sparingly—white space preferred

## Voice and Tone

### Brand Voice Characteristics

| Characteristic | Meaning | Example |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| Clear | Jargon-free, accessible | "Monthly income" not "UBI disbursement" |
| Confident | Assured, not arrogant | "This will work" not "This might work" |
| Human | Warm, personal | "You deserve security" not "Citizens will receive" |
| Hopeful | Positive vision | "Future we can build" not "Crisis we must avert" |
| Honest | Acknowledge complexity | "This is challenging but achievable" |

### Tone Variations

**Formal (Policy, Legal):**
- More technical language acceptable
- Careful, precise statements
- Citations and evidence
- Third person perspective

**Standard (General Communications):**
- Accessible language
- Active voice
- Second person ("you") engagement
- Balanced evidence and emotion

**Casual (Social, Community):**
- Conversational tone
- Shorter sentences
- Humor appropriate (carefully)
- First person plural ("we")

**Urgent (Fundraising, Mobilization):**
- Action-oriented
- Emotional appeals
- Specific asks
- Time-bound language

## Usage Rights

### Internal Use

All brand assets may be used by:
- Staff for official communications
- Approved volunteers for sanctioned activities
- Chapters for local organizing (with guidance)
- Candidates with endorsement

### External Use

Require approval for:
- Media use of logos
- Partner co-branding
- Merchandise production
- Third-party publications

### Prohibited Use

Never permit:
- Commercial use without agreement
- Modification of logo
- Use implying endorsement without approval
- Use on materials inconsistent with values
- Use by opposing candidates or organizations
