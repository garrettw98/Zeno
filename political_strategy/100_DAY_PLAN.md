# The Human Standard: First 100 Days
## Executive Action Plan for a New Administration

---

## Vision

The first 100 days of a Human Standard administration set the trajectory for transforming America's relationship with technology and ensuring broad-based prosperity. While major legislative achievements require Congress, the executive branch can immediately begin implementing core principles through executive orders, agency directives, and administrative action.

---

## Guiding Principles for Day One

1. **Move Fast**: Political capital diminishes; use it immediately
2. **Show, Don't Just Tell**: Tangible actions over symbolic gestures
3. **Build Coalitions**: Every action should expand, not shrink, support
4. **Prepare for Backlash**: Anticipate opposition and have responses ready
5. **Set Up Legislative Wins**: Executive actions that create momentum for laws

---

## Day One Actions

### Executive Order 1: Establishing the National AI Coordination Office

**Summary**: Create the central coordinating body for federal AI policy within the Executive Office of the President.

**Key Provisions**:
- Establish NAICO as principal advisor to President on AI policy
- Appoint National AI Director (Cabinet-level rank)
- Begin federal AI system inventory
- Require agency AI governance plans within 90 days

**Immediate Effect**: Signals priority, creates institutional home.

---

### Executive Order 2: AI Transparency for Federal Systems

**Summary**: Require transparency and accountability for AI systems used by federal agencies to make decisions affecting the public.

**Key Provisions**:
- All federal AI systems affecting individual rights must be inventoried
- Agencies must disclose when AI is used in consequential decisions
- Citizens have right to explanation of AI-assisted decisions
- Bias testing required within 180 days for existing systems
- New AI deployments require impact assessments

**Immediate Effect**: Makes federal government model for AI accountability.

---

### Executive Order 3: Strategic Digital Asset Position

**Summary**: Direct Treasury to begin developing a strategic position in Bitcoin and establish clear digital asset policy.

**Key Provisions**:
- Treasury to retain (not auction) seized Bitcoin
- Federal Reserve to study strategic reserve implementation
- SEC, CFTC, Treasury to issue joint regulatory clarity guidance within 90 days
- Explicit protection for self-custody rights
- No CBDC development for retail use without Congressional authorization

**Immediate Effect**: Establishes sound money credentials, provides regulatory clarity signal.

---

### Executive Order 4: Automation Impact Assessment

**Summary**: Require assessment of automation's impact on federal workforce and economy.

**Key Provisions**:
- Labor Department to publish quarterly automation displacement estimates
- Federal agencies to assess automation's impact before major procurement
- GAO to study automation's fiscal impact (tax base, program costs)
- Commerce Department to establish Automation Economy Advisory Council

**Immediate Effect**: Creates data infrastructure for policy development.

---

### Executive Order 5: Open Source Government AI

**Summary**: Require government-developed AI systems to be open source by default.

**Key Provisions**:
- All AI systems developed with federal funds presumptively open source
- Agencies must justify classification exceptions
- Establish National AI Repository for government code sharing
- Contractors must open-source government-specific AI components
- Promote international open-source AI cooperation

**Immediate Effect**: Establishes transparency standard, enables collaboration.

## Risk Mitigation

### If Congress Stalls

**Backup Plans:**
- Maximize executive authority
- State partnerships become primary vehicle
- Regulatory action where possible
- Prepare for next Congress

**Specific Actions:**
- Citizen's Royalty pilots via emergency authority
- AI governance via procurement power
- Bitcoin reserve via existing Treasury authority
- Regulatory clarity via agency guidance

### If Opposition Attacks

**Rapid Response:**
- War room for message discipline
- Validator voices pre-positioned
- Opposition research ready
- Alternative framings prepared

**Key Attack Responses:**
- "Socialism": Point to Milton Friedman, Alaska, bipartisan support
- "Anti-innovation": Point to R&D exemptions, tech leader support
- "Unaffordable": Point to revenue sources, cost of inaction
- "Government overreach": Point to limited scope, deregulatory elements

### If Economic Crisis

**Opportunity Framing:**
- "This is exactly why we need economic security"
- Accelerate Citizen's Royalty pilot as emergency response
- Position Human Standard as solution

**Risk Management:**
- Don't over-promise on timing
- Maintain fiscal credibility
- Have contingency policies ready

## The 100-Day Scorecard

### What Success Looks Like

**Excellent (A):**
- 2+ major bills signed into law
- All key Cabinet confirmed
- All executive orders fully implemented
- 60%+ public approval of agenda
- International agreements secured

**Good (B):**
- 1 major bill signed
- Most Cabinet confirmed
- Executive orders on track
- 50%+ public approval
- International progress

**Acceptable (C):**
- Major bills through committee
- Core Cabinet confirmed
- Executive orders proceeding
- 45%+ public approval
- Foundation laid

**Concerning (D):**
- Legislative stall
- Confirmation fights ongoing
- Executive action challenged
- Below 40% approval
- Significant setbacks

### Recovery Strategy

If at Day 100 things are not on track:

1. **Diagnose**: What went wrong?
2. **Adjust**: Change tactics, not goals
3. **Refocus**: Narrow to achievable targets
4. **Rebuild**: Shore up coalition
5. **Persist**: 100 days is beginning, not end
