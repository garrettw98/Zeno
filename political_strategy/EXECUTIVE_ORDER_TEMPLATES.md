## Executive Order Template 1
### Establishing the National AI Coordination Office

---

**EXECUTIVE ORDER**

**ESTABLISHING THE NATIONAL AI COORDINATION OFFICE**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, and to ensure the responsible development and deployment of artificial intelligence in a manner that protects American citizens, strengthens democratic accountability, and promotes broadly shared prosperity, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**Section 1. Policy.**

It is the policy of the United States that:

(a) Artificial intelligence systems that affect the rights, opportunities, or wellbeing of Americans must be transparent, accountable, and subject to democratic oversight.

(b) The benefits of artificial intelligence should be broadly shared across American society, not concentrated among a narrow elite.

(c) Humans must remain in meaningful control of decisions that significantly affect individuals' lives, with AI serving as a tool to augment rather than replace human judgment in consequential matters.

(d) The Federal Government should lead by example in the responsible development and deployment of AI systems.

(e) American leadership in AI governance can establish international norms that reflect democratic values.

**Section 2. Establishment of the National AI Coordination Office.**

(a) There is established within the Executive Office of the President the National AI Coordination Office (NAICO).

(b) The mission of NAICO is to coordinate Federal policy on artificial intelligence, ensure the responsible use of AI by Federal agencies, protect citizens affected by AI systems, and advise the President on matters relating to artificial intelligence.

**Section 3. Director of NAICO.**

(a) NAICO shall be headed by a Director, who shall be appointed by the President. The Director shall serve at the pleasure of the President.

(b) The Director shall have the rank and status of the head of an Executive department and shall be compensated at the rate prescribed for Level II of the Executive Schedule.

(c) The Director shall report directly to the President and shall serve as the principal advisor to the President on matters relating to artificial intelligence policy.

**Section 4. Functions of NAICO.**

(a) The Director shall:

(i) Develop and coordinate the implementation of Federal AI policy;

(ii) Advise the President on AI-related matters, including technological developments, policy implications, and governance challenges;

(iii) Oversee Federal agency compliance with AI governance requirements established by executive order or statute;

(iv) Chair the interagency AI Policy Council established in Section 6;

(v) Represent the United States in international discussions on AI governance;

(vi) Coordinate the Federal Government's engagement with the private sector, academia, and civil society on AI matters;

(vii) Publish annual reports on the state of AI in Federal Government and the economy;

(viii) Establish and manage a National AI Repository for Federal AI systems as described in Section 8;

(ix) Receive and review complaints from citizens regarding Federal AI systems; and

(x) Perform such other functions as the President may direct.

**Section 5. Structure of NAICO.**

(a) NAICO shall include:

(i) Office of Standards and Policy, responsible for developing AI governance standards and policy recommendations;

(ii) Office of Repository Management, responsible for operating the National AI Repository;

(iii) Office of Compliance and Audit, responsible for ensuring Federal agency compliance; and

(iv) Office of Citizen Rights, responsible for receiving complaints, facilitating citizen access to information, and ensuring redress.

(b) The Director may establish additional offices and organizational units as necessary to carry out the functions of NAICO.

**Section 6. AI Policy Council.**

(a) There is established the AI Policy Council, which shall advise the President on AI policy matters and coordinate AI policy across the Federal Government.

(b) The Council shall be chaired by the Director of NAICO and shall include:

(i) The Secretary of Commerce;
(ii) The Secretary of Defense;
(iii) The Secretary of Health and Human Services;
(iv) The Secretary of Homeland Security;
(v) The Secretary of Labor;
(vi) The Secretary of the Treasury;
(vii) The Attorney General;
(viii) The Director of the Office of Management and Budget;
(ix) The Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy;
(x) The Chair of the Council of Economic Advisers;
(xi) The Director of National Intelligence; and
(xii) Such other officials as the President may designate.

(c) The Council shall meet at least quarterly and shall:

(i) Coordinate AI policy across agencies;
(ii) Resolve interagency disputes on AI matters;
(iii) Review major AI deployments before approval; and
(iv) Advise the Director on policy priorities.

**Section 7. Agency AI Officers.**

(a) Within 60 days of this order, the head of each Executive department and agency shall designate an AI Officer with responsibility for AI governance within the agency.

(b) Agency AI Officers shall:

(i) Report to the agency head on AI matters;
(ii) Coordinate with NAICO on compliance matters;
(iii) Maintain the agency's inventory of AI systems;
(iv) Ensure agency AI systems meet governance requirements; and
(v) Receive and respond to citizen inquiries regarding agency AI systems.

(c) Agency AI Officers shall meet quarterly with the Director of NAICO.

**Section 8. National AI Repository.**

(a) NAICO shall establish and maintain a National AI Repository for Federal AI systems.

(b) The Repository shall include:

(i) Registration of all AI systems used by Federal agencies for consequential decisions;
(ii) Source code for AI systems developed with Federal funds (subject to security classifications);
(iii) Documentation of training data, methodologies, and performance metrics;
(iv) Bias testing results and mitigation measures;
(v) Usage statistics and outcome data; and
(vi) Citizen-accessible summaries of AI systems affecting the public.

(c) Unless an exception is granted pursuant to Section 9, AI systems registered in the Repository shall be made available for public inspection.

**Section 9. Classification and Exceptions.**

(a) Information in the National AI Repository may be classified or restricted only if:

(i) Public disclosure would compromise national security;
(ii) Public disclosure would compromise ongoing law enforcement operations; or
(iii) An exception is specifically authorized by statute.

(b) Requests for classification or restriction must be submitted in writing to the Director, who shall consult with the AI Policy Council before making a determination.

(c) Classification determinations shall be reviewed annually and shall not extend beyond five years without renewal.

(d) Even where source code is classified, agencies must provide citizen-accessible explanations of how AI systems function and make decisions.

**Section 10. Federal AI System Inventory.**

(a) Within 90 days of this order, each Executive department and agency shall submit to NAICO a comprehensive inventory of AI systems used by the agency for consequential decisions affecting members of the public.

(b) The inventory shall include, for each system:

(i) Name and description of the system;
(ii) Purpose and decision domain;
(iii) Vendor or developer;
(iv) Date of deployment;
(v) Number of decisions or actions taken using the system;
(vi) Categories of individuals affected;
(vii) Training data sources;
(viii) Known limitations and failure modes; and
(ix) Human oversight procedures.

(c) NAICO shall publish a consolidated inventory within 120 days of this order, with appropriate classifications for security-sensitive systems.

**Section 11. Implementation.**

(a) The Director of the Office of Management and Budget shall allocate such funds as are necessary to establish NAICO and carry out this order.

(b) Executive departments and agencies shall cooperate with NAICO and provide such information and assistance as the Director may request.

(c) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

**Section 12. General Provisions.**

(a) Nothing in this order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect the authority granted by law to an executive department or agency, or the head thereof.

(b) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

(c) This order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States, its departments, agencies, or entities, its officers, employees, or agents, or any other person.

**THE WHITE HOUSE,**
**[DATE]**

## Executive Order Template 2
### Transparency and Accountability for Federal AI Systems

---

**EXECUTIVE ORDER**

**ENSURING TRANSPARENCY AND ACCOUNTABILITY FOR ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS IN FEDERAL GOVERNMENT**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**Section 1. Purpose.**

Artificial intelligence systems are increasingly used by Federal agencies to make decisions affecting the rights, opportunities, and wellbeing of Americans. These decisions include eligibility for benefits, enforcement actions, risk assessments, and allocation of resources. Americans have a right to understand when AI systems affect their lives and to challenge decisions that may be erroneous or unjust. This order establishes requirements for transparency, accountability, and human oversight of Federal AI systems.

**Section 2. Definitions.**

For purposes of this order:

(a) "Algorithmic Decision System" means any computational process, including one derived from machine learning, statistics, or other data processing or artificial intelligence techniques, that makes, recommends, or informs decisions affecting the rights, opportunities, or access of individuals to programs, benefits, services, or information.

(b) "Consequential Decision" means a decision that has a significant effect on an individual's life circumstances, including decisions affecting:

(i) Benefits, services, or programs administered by the Federal Government;
(ii) Employment with the Federal Government or Federal contractors;
(iii) Enforcement actions, including audits, investigations, and sanctions;
(iv) Immigration status, detention, or removal;
(v) Access to credit, housing, or other federally-regulated opportunities; and
(vi) Any other determination affecting individual rights or interests.

(c) "Covered Agency" means an Executive department or agency.

**Section 3. Right to Know.**

(a) Any individual subject to a Consequential Decision made, recommended, or informed by an Algorithmic Decision System shall have the right to:

(i) Know that an Algorithmic Decision System was used in the decision;
(ii) Receive a plain-language explanation of how the system functions;
(iii) Understand the key factors that influenced the decision in their case;
(iv) Know whether and how they can provide additional information; and
(v) Request human review of the decision.

(b) Covered Agencies shall notify individuals at the time of a Consequential Decision that an Algorithmic Decision System was used and shall provide access to the information described in subsection (a).

**Section 4. Right to Explanation.**

(a) Individuals subject to Consequential Decisions have the right to receive, upon request, an explanation of:

(i) The specific factors considered in their case;
(ii) The relative importance of each factor;
(iii) How changes in their circumstances might affect the outcome; and
(iv) The confidence level of the system's recommendation.

(b) Explanations shall be:

(i) Written in plain language understandable to a layperson;
(ii) Provided within 30 days of request;
(iii) Available in languages other than English where required by existing law; and
(iv) Accessible to individuals with disabilities.

**Section 5. Right to Human Review.**

(a) Any individual subject to an adverse Consequential Decision made, recommended, or informed by an Algorithmic Decision System shall have the right to request human review.

(b) Upon request, a qualified human reviewer shall:

(i) Review the individual's case de novo;
(ii) Consider any additional information provided by the individual;
(iii) Have authority to override the algorithmic recommendation; and
(iv) Provide a written explanation of the final decision.

(c) Human review shall be completed within 30 days of request, or within a shorter time where required by statute or regulation.

**Section 6. Bias Testing and Mitigation.**

(a) Before deploying an Algorithmic Decision System for Consequential Decisions, Covered Agencies shall:

(i) Conduct bias testing to assess whether the system produces disparate outcomes based on race, color, national origin, sex, religion, age, disability, or other protected characteristics;
(ii) Document the results of bias testing;
(iii) Develop and implement mitigation measures for identified disparities; and
(iv) Submit bias testing results and mitigation plans to NAICO.

(b) After deployment, Covered Agencies shall:

(i) Conduct ongoing monitoring for bias;
(ii) Report annually on system performance across demographic groups; and
(iii) Address newly identified disparities promptly.

(c) NAICO shall develop standards for bias testing within 90 days of this order, including:

(i) Statistical thresholds for identifying disparities;
(ii) Required demographic categories for testing;
(iii) Methodologies for testing and validation; and
(iv) Remediation requirements for systems that fail to meet standards.

**Section 7. Human-in-the-Loop Requirements.**

(a) No Algorithmic Decision System shall make a final Consequential Decision without meaningful human oversight, except where:

(i) Human review of each decision is not feasible due to volume;
(ii) The system has been validated through bias testing;
(iii) Robust monitoring and sampling procedures are in place; and
(iv) Individuals retain the right to request human review.

(b) For decisions affecting liberty interests (including detention, removal, or criminal enforcement), human review of each decision is required regardless of volume.

(c) Human reviewers shall:

(i) Have adequate training on the system's limitations;
(ii) Have authority and practical ability to override recommendations;
(iii) Be evaluated on the quality of their review, not on agreement with the system; and
(iv) Document the basis for any override.

**Section 8. Impact Assessments.**

(a) Before deploying a new Algorithmic Decision System or significantly modifying an existing system, Covered Agencies shall complete an Algorithmic Impact Assessment.

(b) Assessments shall include:

(i) Description of the system and its intended purpose;
(ii) Data sources and training methodologies;
(iii) Expected benefits and potential risks;
(iv) Bias testing results;
(v) Human oversight procedures;
(vi) Citizen notification and appeal procedures; and
(vii) Ongoing monitoring plans.

(c) Assessments shall be submitted to NAICO and, except for classified systems, made publicly available.

**Section 9. Open Source Requirements.**

(a) Algorithmic Decision Systems developed with Federal funds shall be open source, unless:

(i) Classification is required for national security; or
(ii) An exception is granted by NAICO based on demonstrated need.

(b) Even where source code is not public, Covered Agencies shall publish:

(i) Documentation of system methodology;
(ii) Performance metrics and validation results;
(iii) Bias testing results; and
(iv) Plain-language descriptions for the public.

(c) Private vendors providing Algorithmic Decision Systems to Covered Agencies shall, as a condition of the contract, agree to disclosure requirements that enable the agency to meet its obligations under this order.

**Section 10. Audit and Accountability.**

(a) The Inspector General of each Covered Agency shall audit compliance with this order annually.

(b) NAICO shall conduct government-wide audits of Algorithmic Decision Systems every two years.

(c) Audit results shall be reported to Congress and made publicly available.

(d) Agency officials responsible for Algorithmic Decision Systems that fail to meet the requirements of this order may be subject to administrative action.

**Section 11. Implementation.**

(a) NAICO shall issue guidance implementing this order within 90 days.

(b) Covered Agencies shall come into compliance with this order within 180 days for new systems and within 365 days for existing systems.

(c) NAICO may grant extensions for existing systems that require substantial modification.

**Section 12. General Provisions.**

[Standard general provisions language]

**THE WHITE HOUSE,**

## Executive Order Template 3
### Establishing a Strategic Digital Asset Position

---

**EXECUTIVE ORDER**

**ESTABLISHING A STRATEGIC DIGITAL ASSET POSITION FOR THE UNITED STATES**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, including the Gold Reserve Act of 1934 as amended and the Treasury and General Government Appropriations Act, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**Section 1. Policy.**

It is the policy of the United States that:

(a) Digital assets, including Bitcoin, represent a significant technological and financial innovation with implications for national security, monetary policy, and economic competitiveness;

(b) The United States should maintain strategic reserves in assets that provide stability, security, and protection against monetary instability;

(c) Americans have the fundamental right to hold and control their own digital assets without government permission or intermediaries;

(d) Regulatory clarity for digital assets promotes innovation, protects consumers, and maintains American leadership in financial technology;

(e) Central Bank Digital Currencies for retail use present unacceptable risks to financial privacy and should not be pursued; and

(f) The United States should lead international efforts to develop sensible digital asset governance.

**Section 2. Diversified Strategic Reserve.**

(a) The Secretary of the Treasury is directed to establish a Diversified Strategic Reserve.

(b) Effective immediately, Bitcoin and other digital assets seized by Federal law enforcement and forfeited to the United States shall not be sold at public auction but shall be transferred to the Diversified Strategic Reserve, unless:

(i) Return to a victim is required by law or court order; or
(ii) The Secretary determines that sale is necessary for other compelling reasons.

(c) The Secretary, in consultation with the Federal Reserve, shall develop a plan for the strategic accumulation of Bitcoin through market purchases, to be submitted to the President within 90 days.

(d) The Diversified Strategic Reserve shall be:

(i) Maintained in secure custody using industry best practices;
(ii) Subject to regular audit by independent auditors;
(iii) Reported quarterly to Congress; and
(iv) Managed with a long-term investment horizon.

(e) The Secretary shall establish the Office of Digital Asset Strategy within the Treasury to manage the Reserve and coordinate digital asset policy.

**Section 3. Regulatory Clarity.**

(a) Within 90 days of this order, the Secretary of the Treasury, the Chairman of the Securities and Exchange Commission, and the Chairman of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission shall jointly issue guidance that:

(i) Provides clear standards for when a digital asset is a security, commodity, or other category;
(ii) Establishes predictable registration and compliance requirements;
(iii) Creates safe harbors for good-faith compliance efforts; and
(iv) Addresses the treatment of decentralized protocols and applications.

(b) The guidance shall prioritize clarity and consistency, enabling innovation while protecting consumers and market integrity.

(c) Agencies shall engage in public rulemaking to codify guidance within one year.

**Section 4. Self-Custody Rights.**

(a) No Federal agency shall:

(i) Require individuals to use custodial services for digital assets;
(ii) Prohibit or restrict the use of non-custodial wallets;
(iii) Mandate disclosure of private keys or wallet addresses except pursuant to lawful legal process;
(iv) Impose burdensome registration requirements on individuals holding digital assets for personal use; or
(v) Block access to public blockchain networks.

(b) The rights of Americans to hold, transfer, and transact in digital assets using self-hosted wallets are affirmed and protected.

**Section 5. Central Bank Digital Currency Prohibition.**

(a) No Federal agency shall develop, test, pilot, or deploy a retail Central Bank Digital Currency without explicit authorization from Congress.

(b) For purposes of this order, a "retail Central Bank Digital Currency" means a digital currency issued directly by the Federal Reserve to individuals for everyday transactions.

(c) This prohibition does not apply to:

(i) Wholesale CBDC systems limited to interbank settlement;
(ii) Research and study of CBDC technologies; or
(iii) Engagement in international CBDC discussions.

(d) Any ongoing retail CBDC projects shall be terminated.

**Section 6. Stablecoin Framework.**

(a) The Secretary of the Treasury shall develop a federal framework for the regulation of stablecoins within 120 days.

(b) The framework shall:

(i) Establish clear licensing requirements;
(ii) Mandate full reserve backing with high-quality assets;
(iii) Require regular attestation and audit of reserves;
(iv) Protect consumer redemption rights;
(v) Coordinate with state regulators; and
(vi) Enable innovation while ensuring stability.

(c) The goal of stablecoin regulation is to extend the reach of the US dollar internationally while maintaining financial stability.

**Section 7. Digital Asset Taxation.**

(a) The Secretary of the Treasury shall issue guidance clarifying the tax treatment of digital asset transactions within 60 days, including:

(i) Treatment of like-kind exchanges;
(ii) Reporting requirements for exchanges and brokers;
(iii) De minimis exemptions for small transactions; and
(iv) Staking, mining, and other novel activities.

(b) The goal of tax guidance is clarity and simplicity, encouraging compliance and enabling everyday use of digital assets.

**Section 8. International Leadership.**

(a) The Secretary of State, Secretary of the Treasury, and United States Trade Representative shall:

(i) Engage allies on digital asset governance;
(ii) Promote American values (privacy, innovation, freedom) in international standards;
(iii) Counter authoritarian approaches to digital currencies; and
(iv) Develop mutual recognition agreements where appropriate.

(b) The United States shall lead efforts to establish international norms consistent with democratic values.

**Section 9. Implementation.**

(a) The Secretary of the Treasury shall coordinate implementation of this order.

(b) Executive departments and agencies shall cooperate with Treasury and provide such information and assistance as may be required.

(c) The Office of Management and Budget shall allocate funds necessary to carry out this order.

**Section 10. General Provisions.**

[Standard general provisions language]

## Executive Order Template 4
### Assessing Automation's Impact on the American Economy and Workforce

---

**EXECUTIVE ORDER**

**ASSESSING THE IMPACT OF AUTOMATION ON THE AMERICAN ECONOMY AND WORKFORCE**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**Section 1. Purpose.**

Automation and artificial intelligence are transforming the American economy at an accelerating pace. While these technologies create opportunities for increased productivity and new industries, they also pose risks of displacement for American workers. Responsible policy requires understanding the scale, pace, and distribution of automation's effects. This order directs the Federal Government to comprehensively assess automation's impact and prepare for the transition.

**Section 2. Definitions.**

(a) "Automation" means the use of technology, including robotics, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and software systems, to perform tasks previously performed by human workers.

(b) "Displacement" means the elimination or substantial reduction of jobs or work hours due to automation.

(c) "Transition" means the process by which workers move from displaced jobs to new employment, including retraining, relocation, and adjustment.

**Section 3. Automation Impact Assessment.**

(a) The Secretary of Labor, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce, the Director of the National Economic Council, and the Chair of the Council of Economic Advisers, shall conduct a comprehensive assessment of automation's impact on the American workforce and economy.

(b) The assessment shall include:

(i) Estimates of jobs at high risk of displacement due to automation over the next 10 and 20 years, by occupation, industry, and region;
(ii) Estimates of new jobs likely to be created by technological advancement;
(iii) Analysis of wage effects of automation on various worker categories;
(iv) Assessment of the adequacy of current workforce development systems;
(v) Analysis of fiscal impacts, including effects on tax revenues and social programs;
(vi) International comparison of automation exposure and policy responses; and
(vii) Recommendations for policy responses.

(c) A preliminary assessment shall be submitted to the President within 180 days. A comprehensive assessment shall be submitted within one year.

(d) The Department of Labor shall thereafter publish quarterly updates on automation trends.

**Section 4. Bureau of Labor Statistics Data Collection.**

(a) The Commissioner of Labor Statistics shall:

(i) Develop and publish regular statistics on automation and its effects on employment;
(ii) Add questions on automation exposure to existing surveys;
(iii) Create an automation displacement index measuring the pace of technological change; and
(iv) Track outcomes for displaced workers over time.

(b) Initial data products shall be available within 180 days.

**Section 5. Federal Procurement.**

(a) Before major procurements involving automation that may displace Federal workers or contractor employees, agencies shall:

(i) Assess the employment impact of the procurement;
(ii) Consider transition assistance for affected workers;
(iii) Report automation-related procurement to NAICO; and
(iv) Ensure compliance with any applicable workforce provisions.

(b) The Federal Acquisition Regulatory Council shall issue guidance implementing this section within 120 days.

**Section 6. Office of Automation Transition.**

(a) The Secretary of Labor shall establish an Office of Automation Transition within the Department of Labor.

(b) The Office shall:

(i) Coordinate workforce development efforts related to automation;
(ii) Administer pilot programs for transition assistance;
(iii) Partner with states on transition initiatives;
(iv) Develop best practices for worker transition; and
(v) Advise the Secretary on automation-related workforce policy.

(c) The Office shall be operational within 120 days.

**Section 7. Pilot Programs.**

(a) The Secretary of Labor, in partnership with interested states, shall establish pilot programs testing approaches to assist workers displaced by automation.

(b) Pilots may include:

(i) Extended income support during transition;
(ii) Intensive retraining programs;
(iii) Relocation assistance;
(iv) Portable benefits systems;
(v) Entrepreneurship support; and
(vi) Early retirement options for older workers.

(c) The Secretary shall report on pilot design within 90 days and launch initial pilots within one year.

**Section 8. Industry Engagement.**

(a) The Secretary of Commerce shall establish an Automation Advisory Council comprising representatives from:

(i) Industries with high automation exposure;
(ii) Technology companies;
(iii) Labor organizations;
(iv) Academic experts;
(v) State and local governments; and
(vi) Civil society organizations.

(b) The Council shall advise the Secretary on automation trends and policy responses.

(c) The Council shall meet at least quarterly and provide annual recommendations.

**Section 9. Interagency Coordination.**

(a) The Director of the Domestic Policy Council shall convene an interagency working group on automation policy, including representatives from:

(i) Department of Labor;
(ii) Department of Commerce;
(iii) Department of Education;
(iv) Department of the Treasury;
(v) Small Business Administration;
(vi) Office of Science and Technology Policy; and
(vii) Council of Economic Advisers.

(b) The working group shall coordinate policy development and report quarterly to the President.

**Section 10. Report to Congress.**

(a) Within one year, and annually thereafter, the President shall submit to Congress a report on:

(i) The state of automation in the American economy;
(ii) Actions taken under this order;
(iii) Recommendations for legislation; and
(iv) Assessment of policy effectiveness.

**Section 11. General Provisions.**

## Executive Order Template 5
### Requiring Open Source for Government AI Systems

---

**EXECUTIVE ORDER**

**PROMOTING OPEN SOURCE DEVELOPMENT OF FEDERAL ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**Section 1. Policy.**

It is the policy of the United States that:

(a) AI systems used by Federal agencies to make decisions affecting the public should be transparent and subject to public scrutiny;

(b) Open source development promotes security, innovation, and accountability;

(c) Taxpayer-funded AI development should benefit the public; and

(d) Open source AI can establish American leadership and values globally.

**Section 2. Open Source Default.**

(a) AI systems developed with Federal funds shall be open source, meaning the source code, training methodologies, and validation procedures shall be publicly available.

(b) This requirement applies to:

(i) AI systems developed by Federal agencies;
(ii) AI systems developed under Federal contracts primarily for government use; and
(iii) Substantial modifications to existing systems made with Federal funds.

(c) This requirement does not apply to:

(i) AI systems where classification is required for national security;
(ii) AI systems for sensitive law enforcement operations where disclosure would compromise effectiveness;
(iii) Commercial off-the-shelf products where only minor customization is involved; or
(iv) Systems granted specific exemption by NAICO.

**Section 3. National AI Repository.**

(a) NAICO shall establish and maintain a National AI Repository for open source Federal AI systems.

(b) The Repository shall include:

(i) Source code for covered AI systems;
(ii) Documentation and training materials;
(iii) Test data and validation results;
(iv) Usage guidelines and best practices;
(v) Issue tracking and community discussion; and
(vi) Version control and change history.

(c) The Repository shall be publicly accessible and shall permit collaboration consistent with security requirements.

(d) The Repository shall be operational within one year.

**Section 4. Procurement Requirements.**

(a) Federal agencies procuring AI systems shall:

(i) Prefer open source solutions where available and adequate;
(ii) Require that custom development be open source unless exempted;
(iii) Ensure contractors grant the government rights to release source code; and
(iv) Consider long-term sustainability and community support.

(b) The Federal Acquisition Regulatory Council shall issue guidance within 120 days.

**Section 5. Exemption Process.**

(a) Agencies may request exemption from open source requirements by submitting a written justification to NAICO explaining:

(i) The specific system and its purpose;
(ii) The reason open source is not feasible;
(iii) Alternative transparency measures to be employed; and
(iv) A timeline for revisiting the exemption.

(b) NAICO shall review requests within 60 days and may grant exemptions for good cause shown.

(c) Exemptions shall be reviewed annually.

**Section 6. Security Considerations.**

(a) Open source release shall not include:

(i) Classified information;
(ii) Training data containing personally identifiable information;
(iii) Information that would compromise security of operating systems; or
(iv) Information specifically prohibited by statute.

(b) Security review shall be conducted before public release.

(c) Open source release should follow responsible disclosure practices.

**Section 7. International Cooperation.**

(a) The Secretary of State shall promote international adoption of open source AI for government systems.

(b) The United States shall seek partnerships with allies on shared open source AI development.

(c) Open source AI can serve as a counter to authoritarian AI development that lacks transparency.

**Section 8. Implementation.**

(a) NAICO shall issue implementing guidance within 90 days.

(b) Agencies shall inventory existing AI systems and identify open source candidates within 180 days.

(c) New AI procurements shall comply with this order immediately.

(d) Existing systems shall transition to compliance within two years.

**Section 9. General Provisions.**

[Standard general provisions language]

**THE WHITE HOUSE,**
**[DATE]**

## Usage Notes

### Legal Considerations

- Executive orders have the force of law for Executive Branch agencies
- Cannot create private rights of action without legislation
- Cannot appropriate funds without Congressional action
- Subject to judicial review
- Can be revoked by subsequent President

### Political Considerations

- Day One orders signal priorities and generate coverage
- Orders should align with campaign promises
- Ambitious orders may face legal challenges
- Some provisions may require legislative follow-up
- Industry and stakeholder engagement affects implementation

### Implementation Priorities

1. **Immediate**: NAICO establishment, seized Bitcoin retention
2. **30 Days**: Agency directives, guidance initiation
3. **90 Days**: Initial standards and frameworks
4. **180 Days**: Full compliance with new requirements
5. **1 Year**: Comprehensive implementation
