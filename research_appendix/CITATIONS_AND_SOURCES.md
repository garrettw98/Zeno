## Part 1: Automation and Employment

### Job Displacement Estimates

**Claim:** AI and automation could displace 30-50% of current jobs within 20 years.

**Sources:**

1. Frey, C. B., & Osborne, M. A. (2017). The future of employment: How susceptible are jobs to computerisation? *Technological Forecasting and Social Change*, 114, 254-280.
   - Finding: 47% of US employment at high risk of automation

2. McKinsey Global Institute. (2017). *A Future That Works: Automation, Employment, and Productivity.*
   - Finding: 50% of work activities technically automatable with current technology

3. Acemoglu, D., & Restrepo, P. (2020). Robots and jobs: Evidence from US labor markets. *Journal of Political Economy*, 128(6), 2188-2244.
   - Finding: Each robot replaces approximately 3.3 workers

4. Autor, D. H., Levy, F., & Murnane, R. J. (2003). The skill content of recent technological change. *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 118(4), 1279-1333.
   - Framework: "Routine" vs. "non-routine" task vulnerability

5. Goldman Sachs. (2023). *The Potentially Large Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Economic Growth.*
   - Finding: Generative AI could expose 300 million jobs globally to automation

**Note on Range:** Estimates vary based on:
- Time horizon considered
- Definition of "displacement" (full vs. partial)
- Assumptions about adoption speed
- Consideration of new job creation

### Automation Is Already Happening

**Industrial Robots:**

6. International Federation of Robotics. (2023). *World Robotics 2023.*
   - Statistics: Global operational stock of industrial robots
   - US robot density: 285 per 10,000 manufacturing employees

**AI Deployment:**

7. Brynjolfsson, E., & Mitchell, T. (2017). What can machine learning do? Workforce implications. *Science*, 358(6370), 1530-1534.
   - Finding: ML suitable for prediction tasks; expanding rapidly

8. McKinsey & Company. (2022). *The State of AI in 2022.*
   - Finding: AI adoption increased from 50% to 56% of companies globally

**Warehouse Automation:**

9. Bessen, J. (2019). Automation and jobs: When technology boosts employment. *Economic Policy*, 34(100), 589-626.
   - Case study: Amazon robotics and warehouse employment

### Wage Effects

**Claim:** Automation contributes to wage stagnation for non-college workers.

10. Autor, D. H. (2019). Work of the past, work of the future. *AEA Papers and Proceedings*, 109, 1-32.
    - Finding: Middle-skill job decline, wage polarization

11. Acemoglu, D., & Autor, D. (2011). Skills, tasks and technologies: Implications for employment and earnings. *Handbook of Labor Economics*, 4, 1043-1171.
    - Framework: Task-based approach to understanding automation effects

12. Economic Policy Institute. (2021). *The Productivity-Pay Gap.*
    - Finding: Productivity up 59.7% since 1979; typical worker compensation up 15.8%

## Part 2: Citizen's Royalty & Income Floor Evidence

### Cash Transfer Studies

**Alaska Permanent Fund:**

13. Jones, D., & Marinescu, I. (2022). The labor market impacts of universal and permanent cash transfers: Evidence from the Alaska Permanent Fund. *American Economic Journal: Economic Policy*, 14(2), 315-340.
    - Finding: No significant employment reduction; 17% increase in part-time work

14. Goldsmith, S. (2012). The Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend: A Case Study in Implementation of a Basic Income Guarantee. *Basic Income Studies*, 7(2).
    - Overview: History and implementation details

**Finland Citizen's Royalty Pilot:**

15. Kangas, O., et al. (2019). *The Basic Income Experiment 2017-2018 in Finland: Preliminary Results.* Ministry of Social Affairs and Health, Finland.
    - Finding: No reduction in employment; significant wellbeing improvements

16. Kangas, O., et al. (2020). *Evaluation of the Finnish Basic Income Experiment.* Kela.
    - Final results: Improved mental wellbeing, trust in institutions, financial security

**Stockton SEED:**

17. West, S., et al. (2021). *Stockton Economic Empowerment Demonstration Preliminary Analysis.* University of Pennsylvania.
    - Finding: Full-time employment increased 12 percentage points vs. control
    - Finding: Reduced anxiety, depression, fatigue

**GiveDirectly Kenya:**

18. Banerjee, A., et al. (2020). Effects of a Universal Basic Income during the Pandemic. Working Paper.
    - Ongoing RCT in Kenya; positive economic activity results

**Meta-Analyses:**

19. Hasdell, R. (2020). What We Know About Universal Basic Income: A Cross-Synthesis of Reviews. *Stanford Basic Income Lab.*
    - Synthesis of multiple studies; generally positive findings

### Work Ethic Concerns

**Claim:** Cash transfers do not significantly reduce work effort.

20. Marinescu, I. (2018). No Strings Attached: The Behavioral Effects of U.S. Unconditional Cash Transfer Programs. *National Bureau of Economic Research*, Working Paper 24337.
    - Finding: Meta-analysis shows minimal work disincentive effects

21. Forget, E. L. (2011). The Town with No Poverty: The Health Effects of a Canadian Guaranteed Annual Income Field Experiment. *Canadian Public Policy*, 37(3), 283-305.
    - Finding: Only new mothers and teenagers significantly reduced work (education)
